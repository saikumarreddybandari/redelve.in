Author Guidelines Information

Types of Articles:
  • Research/ Original Articles (Full Length)
  • Review Articles
  • Brief Reports
  • Case Reports
  • Short Communication
  • Opinion Paper
  • Letter to Editor
  • Editorial
Contact Information:

If you have any questions about the submission process, please contact the Editorial Office by email at contact@redelve.com or by phone at (614) 927-0434. If you are having trouble logging in, please visit https://www.redelve.com/ for more information

Communication about manuscripts occurs primarily through email and only with corresponding authors, so it is important for authors to provide current/working contact information (Address, Institution, Phone numbers, and Email address).

Author Checklist:

Please ensure that the following criteria have been met prior to submission of the Manuscript:

  1. Manuscripts should contain original and authentic results, data and ideas, which are not published elsewhere.

  2. Publishing of an article elsewhere implies that the work described has not been published previously (except in the form of an abstract, a published lecture or academic thesis, check 'Multiple, redundant or concurrent publication' for more information), that it is not under consideration for publication elsewhere, that its publication is approved by all authors and tacitly or explicitly by the responsible authorities where the work was carried out.its copyright should be transferred to the publisher, that the manuscript will not be published elsewhere in the same form, in English or in any other language, including electronically without the written consent of the copyright-holder and that any costs associated with obtaining this permission is the authors' responsibility.

  3. Designate one author as the corresponding author with contact details.

  4. Provide at least 2-6 Taxonomy topics on the submission form.

  5. Precise is 35 words or fewer and uploaded as the “Abstract” file.

  6. Manuscript file must contain a title page, an abstract and continuous line numbering. Abstract must conform to the journal’s structured format

  7. Figures and tables must not be embedded in the manuscript file; submit them as separate files and properly labeled. Tables must include titles, brief description, and footnotes.

  8. Appropriate callouts must be added to the manuscript file.

Important Considerations :

  1. Use the 'active voice' when writing the manuscript.

  2. Spell out acronyms.

  3. Spell-check and grammar-check your manuscript prior to submission.

  4. Ensure that references are in the correct AMA style format.

  5. Make certain that all references are cited in the text.

  6. Obtain permission for use of copyrighted material from other sources.

Preparation of Manuscript:

Manuscripts should be prepared on a word processor or should be typewritten, with all materials double-spaced, with justified alignment, and with suitable margins. All pages should be numbered, starting with page 1, the title page. Tables and figures should be numbered appropriately, no table and figure should be missed, and legends should be written with the same table or figure after giving single space. Tables and figures should be cited well in the text.

Cover Page:

The following must be included:

  1. Article Title

  2. Article Type (Research, Review, Case Report, Short Communication, Editorial, Commentary, Mini-Review, Letter to the editor, etc.)

  3. Name of all authors and their Affiliations

  4. Corresponding author details

  5. Acknowledgments

  6. References

  1. 1 . Title/Heading:

    Suitable to the subject and not irrelevant to its detail work.

  2. 2 . Author affiliation:

    Respective Author Full Name, Department/Institution/University, City should be drafted with the article.

  3. 3 . Corresponding Author:

    The corresponding author is the person responsible for submission and all communications regarding the journal submission. The corresponding author must notify the editors and editorial office, via the submission form.

    Full Name, Department/Institution/University, City, State, Country, Telephone, Fax, and Email.

  4. 4. Abstract
    • Objective or Purpose:Concisely state the study goal.
    • Design:Identify the study design using a phrase such as cross-sectional study, clinical trial, cohort study, etc. Study design types are summarized in the Study Design section of this guide. The CONSORT Worksheet is required for randomized controlled trials.
    • Subjects, Participants, and/or Controls:Describe the persons or eyes studied and the controls if a separate control group is included.
    • Methods, Intervention, or Testing:Describe the principal treatment(s), procedure(s), test(s), or observation(s) performed.
    • Main Outcome Measures:Define the main parameter(s) being measured (e.g., intraocular pressure, visual acuity, a degree of inflammation, etc.)
    • Results:Summarize the principal measurements (data) obtained.
    • Conclusions:State the conclusion(s) derived from the data analysis
  5. 5. Keywords:

    In alphabetical order, separated with semicolons and exhibit appeal with respect to the subject.

  6. 6. The text includes the following
    • Introduction: States the reason for the research: identifies and discusses the findings of others, includes a review of the literature.
    • Materials and Methods:Describes the research population or study sample, describes the method used to gather information or data collection and describes the means used for measuring or measures.
    • Results:Summarizes the results and present findings using text, charts, graphs and tables, Legends for tables, charts, graphs, and figures.
    • Discussion:Analyzes findings, explains the significance of the research, and suggests future projects.
    • References:An alphabetical listing of works that were cited and used to support the research.
  7. 7. Figures and Tables
    Figures

    Suggested formats for figures should be in bitmap formats (JPEG, GIF, TIFF, etc.). Please send us the Photoshop indexes, in case the pictures need any differential segments on distinctive layers.

    Figure Legends

    Use numerical to designate figures (e.g., Figure 1). Use justifiable description, if necessary

    Figure Quality

    Figures should be clear as HD images and should contain figure legends and should be cited properly in the text.

    During submissions, figures are supposed to be at sufficient quality, preferably as JPEGs

    Tables

    Tables should be prepared in MS Excel and should contain table legends and should be cited properly in the text.

    Tables can be included in the text, if not submitted as a separate file, oriented in portrait form (not landscape) and upright on the page, not sideways.

    Table Legends

    Use numerical to designate tables (e.g., Table 1). Use justifiable description, if necessary

    Equations as Graphics

    If equations cannot be encoded in MathML, submit them in TIFF or EPS format as discrete files (i.e., a file containing only the data for one equation). Only when tables cannot be encoded as XML/SGML can they be submitted as graphics. If this method is used, it is critical that the font size in all equations and tables is consistent and legible throughout all submissions.

    • Suggested Equation Extraction Method
    • Table Specifications
    • Equation Specifications
    • Supplementary Information
    • Discrete items of the Supplementary Information (Materials & Methods, Figures, Tables, etc.,) referred to at an appropriate point in the main text of the chapter. Summary diagram/figure included as part of the Supplementary Information (optional). All Supplementary Information is supplied as a single PDF file, where possible. File size within the permitted limits for Supplementary Information. Images should be a maximum size of 640 x 480 pixels (9 x 6.8 inches at 72 pixels per inch).
  8. 8. Acknowledgments:

    Includes affirmation of individuals, grant details, funds, and conflict of interest.

    Format preferably in Word? Processing i.e. Microsoft Word (DOC, DOCX).

  9. 9. References:

    All the references should be cited well in the text.

    References Sample Format:
    • Klopp M, Pfannschmidt J, Dienemann H (2008) Behandlung des Pleuraempyems. Chirurg 79(1): 83-96.
    • Tasci S, Burghard A, Schäfer H, Rabe C, Eternal S, et al. (2005) Langzeitergebnisse der intrapleuralen Fibrinolytikatherapie komplizierter parapneumonischer Pleuraergüsse und Empyeme über kleinlumige Katheter. Medizinische Klinik 100: 181-185.
Plagiarism Policy

Plagiarism is copying another person’s text or ideas and passing the copied material as your own work. Whether Accidental or Unintentional, plagiarism is not only illegal but also morally corrosive.

It is the author's responsibility to make certain that they understand the difference between quoting and paraphrasing, as well as the proper way to cite and delineate quoted material.

All manuscripts received towards ReDelve journals are scanned for plagiarism. If potential plagiarism is detected, authors will be contacted for clarification and/or changes.

Corrections:

Suggestions from the authors, before or after the publication process, are acceptable and ensure that the corrections are clearly legible.

Proofs and Reprints:

Electronic galley proofs (PDF File) will be sent as an attachment to the corresponding author email address. It will be considered as the final version of the manuscript. Only typographical or minor clerical errors will be made in the manuscript at the stage of galley proof. Authors will have free access to the article. Authors can freely download the PDF file from which they can print unlimited copies of their articles and also, they can ask the Journal coordinators for reprints.

Manuscript Withdrawal Policy

Author may wish to withdraw his/her manuscript after submitting it. Changing one’s mind is an author’s prerogative. The author is free to withdraw an article at no charge – as long as it is withdrawn within 10 days of its initial submission.

Processing Fees

ReDelve International is an open access publishing organization and there is no paper, no mailing, no subscriptions, and no restriction against using and sharing articles published in our journals. We do not have funding/support from any Private Organizations/Government.

Our highly specialized Editorial team handles the end to end process from submission to publishing. In addition, articles need graphics and formatting in order to meet rigorous academic standards. Our IT support team helps maintain our online repositories and keep the website up and running. It is for the above reasons and in order to support our teams, that we charge article processing fees. Article processing fees may vary from journal to journal.

For any queries or any discussions regarding Author Guidelines, please contact us at contact@redelve.com
